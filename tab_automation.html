Ore Extractor:

Ore Extractors are used to extract resources from Ore Deposists.
Ore Extractors should be above the Deposit Block to start working.

ⓘ Deposits can have 3 different qualities, which can make outputs different

Quality:
Pure: x1.5
Normal: x1
Impure: x0.5

ඞ Different Deposits have different ammount of ores that can be extracted before getting destroyed

Quantity:
Elevated: "1001 - 1200" | x1
High: "601 - 1000" | x2
Medium: "301 - 600" | x3
Low: "1-150" | x1
Scarce: "1-25" | x3

Amethyst: Low
Coal: Elevated
Iron: High
Gold: High
Redstone: Medium
Emerald: Scarce
Lapis Lazuli: Medium
Diamond: Scarce
Quartz: Medium
Debris: Scarce

ⓘ Amethyst, Coal, Iron, Gold, Redstone, Emerald, Lapis Lazuli and Diamond Deposits can be found in the Overworld. Quartz and Ancient Debris Deposists can be found in the Nether. There are no Deposists in The End.

ⓘ All Deposits have the same Generation Rates - Dev Note: "One day, it will be all different".

ⓘ Deposists status can be only seen with /data block X Y Z - Dev Note: "In the near future, there will be an item to see all the Deposit Information without the use of commands."

Gas Extractor:

Gas Extractors are used to extract gases from Gas Blocks and Atmospheres.
Gas Extractors should be below the Gas Block to start working and with a clear block above to extract gases from atmospheres.

There are currently two Gases:
- Natural Gas
- Sulfur Gas

Natural Gases can be found at Oil Deposists.
Sulfur Gases can't be found anywhere.

Natural Gases:
- Methane
- Ethane
- Propane
- Butane
- Helium

Atmospheres Gases:
- Nitrogen
- Oxygen
- Carbon Dioxide
- Argon
- Water Vapor

ⓘ Dev Note: "In the future, it will be possible to extract different gases from different dimensions atmospheres, meaning new gases comming soon. "

Fluid Extractor:

Fluid Extractors are used to extract fluid from Fluid Blocks. Make sure the Fluid is below the Extractor

Fluid Blocks:
- Water
- Lava
- Oil

Fluid Extractors output a specific type of item called: "Stored Fluid"

ⓘ Dev Note: "In the Future it will be available to transform stored fluids into buckets."
